# Triggers for the Privacy Review for the Release

The Data Protection and Privacy Review is triggered automatically and is completed for the release in the following cases: and Data Privacy Requirements are scoped for the release.

1.  New Product is created in PICASso and a release is created for this product  
    *Trigger:* 1st release created in PICASso. Toggle button ‘Data Protection & Privacy’ should be activated on the ‘Product Details’ tab.

<img src="/media/image42.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.73958in" />

2.  Data Protection and Privacy Risk is changed  
    *Trigger:* Data Protection & Privacy Risk level is changed (to a higher value) compared to the previous release.  
    **Note**: If both the Data Protection & Privacy Risk remains unchanged from the previous release and there are no open actions from the previous release, the Data Protection and Privacy Review tab is still visible in the ‘View’ mode.

<img src="/media/image43.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.70833in" />

3.  Open Actions in Data Protection and Privacy Review  
    *Trigger:* the new release is created based on the completed release with the DPR having open action items so that the Privacy Reviewer can validate if the actions are closed in the new release and the Data Protection & Privacy Risk might be re-assessed.  
    **Note:** If both the Data Protection & Privacy Risk remains unchanged from the previous release and there are no open actions from this previous release, the Data Protection and Privacy Review tab is available in the view mode.

<img src="media/image13.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.15625in" />

4.  Annual Data Review (for Data Protection & Privacy Risk = High, in conjunction with the BU Security Officer - review is required every year).  
    *Trigger:* Release is created in a year after the previous Data Protection and Privacy Review completion (the date of the related FCSR closure date or the date of Data Protection and Privacy Profile update), where the Data Protection & Privacy Risk was assessed as High.

5.  The Data Protection and Privacy Review can also be re-initiated manually and not related to the release in the following cases:

5.1. Incident

5.2. Result of an Audit  
*Trigger:* Update is performed from the Product details in the Data Protection and Privacy Profile section.

# Stages of the Privacy Review in PICASso

The Privacy Review in PICASso tool goes through the following stages:

## 3. Data Protection and Privacy Review Flow

## 3.1. Create and Scope

### 3.1.1. Create the release

To trigger the Data Protection and Privacy Review, the Product Team (Product Owner and/or Security Manager) should create the release for the product by completing the "Create New release" pop-up form or by creating a copy of an existing release via cloning it.

When the Release is created and submitted:  
1) Product Team representative is redirected to the "Release details" tab of the release management flow and Product team can review and update it (if needed).

<img src="/media/image45.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.71875in" />  
2) The user also see 2 more active tabs: "Roles & Responsibilities" and "Questionnaire".

### 3.1.2. Review and specify user for the Responsible Roles

The Product team reviews the list of users assigned to the Roles involved into the release management flow on the "Roles & Responsibilities" tab.

For the Data Protection and Privacy Review, two more roles are added to the previously defined list:

### 3.1.3. Complete the Questionnaire

To define the set of the requirements applicable for the release, Risk Classification, Data Protection & Privacy Risk, and Privacy Sections, the Product Team completes the Questionnaire.

For the Data Protection & Privacy Review, an additional section is added to the existing questionnaire and conditional logic defined based on the answers provided.

Based on the answers provided for the Questionnaire and the product/release details, the following actions are performed by the system:

Data Protection & Privacy Risk level is calculated (Risk calculation is configured in the BackOffice)

Tabs "Process Requirements" and "Product Requirements" become active.

Sets of Privacy Product Requirements and Process Requirements are scoped.

The "Data Protection and Privacy Review" tab visibility for the release is defined.

A set of the Applicable Privacy Sections, the initial Maturity Level for the Privacy Sections, Questions list and Required Evidences are defined.  
Note: But the tab "Data Protection and Privacy Review" remains not activated yet in the release flow.

### 3.1.4. Review and Update the Requirements scoped

When the initial requirements are scoped for the release, the Product team can review the list of Requirements and mark any of them as not applicable (by changing their status) or Add new requirements from the list of not yet selected (by activating the "Show Not Selected Requirements" toggle and clicking on the Add button for the requirements with status "Not Selected").

<img src="media/image18.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.19792in" />

### 3.1.5. Submit the release for review or Cancel

#### 3.1.5.1. Submit for Review

After all the above-mentioned data is provided and the list of requirements is reviewed and updated, the Product Team should submit the release scope to the Dedicated Privacy Advisor for confirmation by clicking the button "Submit for Review", that would trigger the notifications' creation (both email and task) for the Dedicated Privacy Advisor and the release is moved to the next stage (Review and Confirm).

#### 3.1.5.2. Cancel

The Product Team can cancel the release by clicking on the "Cancel Release" button, and all the activities linked to this release will be cancelled as well. The cancellation triggers email notifications for all parties involved.

<img src="/media/image47.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.70833in" />

### 3.1.6. Required Fields on this stage

At this stage, the Product Team should provide the data for the following tabs of the Release Management flow:

- Release Details (all mandatory fields);

- Roles and Responsibilities (Users for the Development Team can be provided (if known), but they are not mandatory yet);

- Questionnaire (all questions shown based on the conditions configured in the BackOffice).

- Process Requirements and Product Requirements tabs, but only the following fields are editable:

1.  "Status" of requirement can be changed (e.g., to "Not Applicable");

2.  Requirements (not scoped for the release automatically) can be manually added to the flow (and removed).

So, in result the following tabs and sections on them are highlighted as recommended (yell sign icon next to the tab’s title) for this stage:

1)  Release Details tab

2)  Roles and Responsibilities tab:

3)  Section "Development Team"

4)  Questionnaire tab

5)  Process Requirements tab

6)  Product Requirements tab

<img src="/media/image48.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.70833in" />

## 3.2. Review and Confirm

**Responsible:**

- for the security part: Security and Data Protection Advisor or LOB Security Leader or BU Security Officer (depending on the Risk Classification for the release and the Minimum Oversight Level);

- for the DPP part: Dedicated Privacy Advisor or Security & Data Protection Advisor if Dedicated Privacy Advisor is not specified.

**Actions for this Stage.** As a part of the Data Protection & Privacy Review, the Dedicated Privacy Advisor gets a task assigned to them in the "My Tasks" list and completes the actions described in the following sections.

### 3.2.1. Review and update the answers provided for the Privacy sections of the Questionnaire

The Dedicated Privacy Advisor or Security & Data Protection Advisor should open the "Questionnaire" tab and check the provided. If it is needed – he/she should update the answers provided and submit this change.

In case the questionnaire answers were updated, based on the updated answers the following points are updated by the system:

Data Protection & Privacy Risk level is re-calculated (Risk calculation is configured in the BackOffice).

The set of the Privacy Product Requirements and Process Requirements are re-scoped.

The "Data Protection and Privacy Review" tab visibility for the release is re-defined.

A set of the Applicable Privacy Sections, the initial Maturity Level for the Privacy Sections, Questions list and Required Evidences are re-defined.  
Note: But the tab "Data Protection and Privacy Review" remains not activated yet in the release flow.

### 3.2.2. Review and update the calculated Data Protection & Privacy Risk value

When the release is on the "Review & Confirm" stage, the initially calculated Data Protection & Privacy Risk can be not only viewed but updated as well.

<img src="media/image21.png" style="width:6.5in;height:0.45833in" />

The Dedicated Privacy Advisor/Security and Data Protection Advisor can update the calculated Data Protection & Privacy Risk manually and provide the justification for this change. When the user clicks on the Data Protection & Privacy Risk classification, he/she is able to select another value for it from the list.

<img src="media/image22.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.125in" />

In case the Risk is manually updated, the following actions are performed by the system:

The set of the Privacy Product Requirements and Process Requirements are re-scoped.

The "Data Protection and Privacy Review" tab visibility for the release is re-defined.

A set of the Applicable Privacy Sections, the initial Maturity Level for the Privacy Sections, Questions list and Required Evidences are re-defined.  
Note: But the tab "Data Protection and Privacy Review" remains not activated yet in the release flow.

Note: Dedicated Privacy Advisor/Security and Data Protection Advisor/Privacy Reviewer cannot change the Risk Classification defined, but only this role is responsible for the Data Protection & Privacy Risk update.

### 3.2.3. Review the list of defined Process and Product Requirements for this release and update it (if needed)

The Dedicated Privacy Advisor/Security and Data Protection Advisor opens the "Process Requirements" and "Product Requirements" tabs one by one and review the list of Privacy requirements scoped for this release (both automatically assigned and those added by the Product Team manually).

During this review, the Dedicated Privacy Advisor/Security and Data Protection Advisor can:

- Update the status of Requirements - by adding the status "Not applicable" (for requirements which are not applicable for this release) or re-opening (changing the status to "Planned") the requirements (marked as "Not Applicable" by the Product Team).

- Add not selected requirements to the scope - by opening the list of Requirements and clicking on the Add icon for the requirement.

<img src="media/image18.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.19792in" />

### 3.2.4. Submit the release to the Manage stage, return it back for Rework or cancel

If the same individual is assigned to the Security and Data Protection Advisor (or LOB Security Leader or BU Security Officer - depending on the Risk Classification for the release and the Minimum Oversight Security Level) and Dedicated Privacy Advisor (if specified) roles, only one task is generated and once the user clicks on the "Confirm" button the release is moved to the next stage. The release is moved to the next stage once all responsible reviewers have submitted their reviews. If the Dedicated Privacy Advisor/ Security and Data Protection Advisor roles are assigned to different users, the release cannot be moved to the next stage until both individuals hit the "Confirm" button.

<img src="media/image23.png" style="width:6.5in;height:1.10417in" />

#### 3.2.4.1. Return to Rework

If one of the reviewers clicks the “Rework” button, the release is automatically moved back to the previous stage, and all review tasks for other reviewers who have not yet clicked “Confirm” are closed until the rework is completed.

If a reviewer has already clicked “Confirn” and the release is later sent for rework, that reviewer’s review remains marked as completed after rework and does not need to be submitted again.

**Note:**

If release stays on the "Security & Privacy Readiness Sign Off" stage for more than **14 days (exceeds SLA)**, it can be moved to the next stage by the BU Security Officer instead of all the users mentioned above.

#### 3.2.4.2. Cancel the release

If any user cancels the release, the following actions are done by the system:

- Release cancellation notification (email) is sent to the Product team, Security and Data Protection Advisor (**or LOB Security Leader or BU Security Officer - depending on the Risk Classification for the release and the Minimum Oversight Security Level**) and Dedicated Privacy Advisor/Security and Data Protection Advisor with justification in the text

- All the open tasks for this release will be closed.

#### 3.2.4.3. Required Fields on this stage

At this stage, the Security and Data Protection Advisor (**or LOB Security Leader or BU Security Officer - depending on the Risk Classification for the release and the Minimum Oversight Security Level**) and Dedicated Privacy Advisor/Security and Data Protection Advisor review and update the data in the following tabs of the Release Management flow:

- Release Details (all mandatory fields);

- Roles and Responsibilities (Users for the Development Team can be updated (if known));

- Questionnaire (all questions shown based on the conditions configured in the BackOffice);

- Data Protection & Privacy Risk (all fields on the pop-up);

- Risk Classification (all fields on the pop-up);

- Process Requirements and Product Requirements tab, but only the following fields are editable:

  - "Status" of requirement can be changed (e.g., to "Not Applicable");

  - Requirements (not scoped for the release automatically) can be manually added to the flow (and removed).

The following tabs and sections on them are highlighted as recommended for this stage:

- Header:

  - Data Protection & Privacy Risk

  - Risk Classification

- Process Requirements tab

- Product Requirements tab

## 3.3. Manage

**Responsible.** Product Team (Product Owner, Security Manager) and Security and Data Protection Advisor on the Product Team behalf

**Actions for this Stage.** As part of the Data Protection and Privacy Review process, at this stage the Product Team is responsible for working on the requirements' management and additional information provisioning on the tab Data Protection and Privacy Review.

Note: the "Data Protection and Privacy Review tab" is activated on this stage with the content scoped after the Data Protection & Privacy Risk calculation/manual update:

### 3.3.1. Manage the requirements

#### 3.3.1.1. Manage the requirements on Product Requirements and Process Requirements pages via the requirement management tool 

If the integrated requirement management tool is used for the requirements of this product (e.g., Jira), the Product Team can submit the requirements to this tool and continue their status update in this integrated tool*.*

**Note:** when the requirement is submitted to the integrated tool, the manual update of its status in PICASso is not possible.

<img src="media/image24.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.17708in" />

#### 3.3.1.2. Manage the requirements on Product Requirements and Process Requirements pages manually

If the integrated requirement management tool is not used for this product (or if the tool is used, but not all the requirements were selected and submitted to the tool) - the requirements are managed manually in PICASso by:

- updating their status;

- providing evidence of completion (as a link);

- adding justification of the status update - required when the status is updated to "Postponed" and "Not applicable".

<img src="media/image25.png" style="width:6.5in;height:3.19792in" />

## 3.3.2. Actions on the Data Protection and Privacy Review tab

If the Privacy Reviewer (or Dedicated Privacy Advisor) has marked that the section is "Not Applicable" at the "Security and Privacy Readiness Sign Off" stage, the Product Team can't make any changes for this section and all the fields are greyed-out.

- submit the action to the issue tracking tool for further work (e.g., to Jira)

When the actions are closed by the Product team, they are submitted to review and validation to the Dedicated Privacy Advisor, so they can confirm that the actions are closed, and no updates are needed.

# 7. Data Protection and Privacy Review Summary and PCC Decision within the FCSR Review

## 7.1. Data Protection and Privacy Review Summary

When the Privacy Reviewer/ Dedicated Privacy Advisor completes the Data Protection and Privacy Review (***on the stage Security and Privacy Readiness Sign Off***) and provide their confirmation of the implemented changes and completed actions, the FCSR decision can be done (but only after the Cybersecurity Residual Risks review was completed as well).

To check and validate the results of completed Data Protection and Privacy Review, the user, responsible for the FCSR decision for this release, sees the table "Data Protection and Privacy Review Summary" with the Maturity Level values defined per each Privacy Section with the arrow sign added next to it determining whether selected level is higher than expected or lower.

By clicking on the title with the Privacy Section, you will be redirected to the respective Privacy Section on the "Data Protection and Privacy Review" tab and can review the detailed information on the work completed for this section and the Review results.

When the Privacy Reviewer/ Dedicated Privacy Advisor completes the Data Protection and Privacy Review (***on the stage Security and Privacy Readiness Sign Off***) and if the Data Protection & Privacy Risk was calculated (or manually updated) as High, the Privacy Reviewer will see PCC Decision section on the "FCSR Decision" tab in addition to the existing content:
